In February 2012, I worked with The Tremor Collective, an experimental theatre group in producing The Rub, directed by Nessa Norich. The Rub is a re-imagining of Hamlet, focusing on the tortured prince's tormented split-personality in a dark and cynical version of the Shakespearean tragedy.

The show was performed over two weekends at Magic Futurebox in Sunset Park, Brooklyn, NY. We designed images and lighting to fill a deep, cavernous space, mapping the projections to the floor, back wall, and eight pillars along the sides. The audience was free to walk on and around the stage, often themselves becoming immersed in projected light, confusing the boundaries between performer and spectator.
