Learning to generate text and audio

Advances in techniques for predicting sequence elements have improved our ability to create generative sequences in various styles. The following are text and audio sequences generated using Long Short Term Memory (LSTM) neural networks in the style of various source texts and audio samples.

I used the char-rnn technique first described and implemented by Andrej Karpathy and re-implemented in Chainer by Yusuke Tomoto to create some text sequences trained on various corpora.

Book of Genesis

State of the union addresses by George W. Bush, 2001-2008

War & Peace by Leo Tolstoy

Some more personal texts were generated trained on my Gmail and on my personal journal.

I also produced audio with Matt Vitelli's GRUV library which trains a recurrent neural network on music to produce new samples of audio in the style of the source audio. Some novel and promising results, but overfitting is evident, with fragments from the source audio being audible in the outputs. More work needs to be done in this area. Some of the outputs:
