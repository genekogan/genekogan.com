<p>
	In February 2012, I worked with <a href="http://www.tremorcollective.tumblr.com">The Tremor Collective</a>, an experimental theater group in producing <i>The Rub</i>, directed by <a href="http://www.nessanorich.com">Nessa Norich</a>. The Rub is a reimagining of Hamlet, focusing on the  prince's tormented split-personality in a cynical, twisted and often hysterical version of the Shakespearean tragedy.
</p>
<p>
	The show was performed over two weekends at <a href="http://www.magicfuturebox.com">Magic Futurebox</a> in Sunset Park, Brooklyn, NY.  We designed images and lighting to fill a deep, cavernous space, mapping the projections to the floor, back wall, and eight pillars along the sides. The audience was invited to walk on and around the stage, often themselves becoming immersed in projected light, blurring the boundaries between performer and spectator.
</p>
